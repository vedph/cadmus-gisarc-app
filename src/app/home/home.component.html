GISARC (Greek in Sicily after the Roman Conquest) is a 2-year research project receiving funding from the European Union’s Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme under the Marie Skłodowska-Curie grant agreement no. 101018554, and hosted at Ca’ Foscari University of Venice (Italy). Livia Tagliapietra is the Principal Investigator.

The project aims to investigate the impact of Roman domination on the Greek language in Sicily through the analysis of all the published Greek, Latin and bilingual inscriptions dating between ca.300 BCE and ca.400 CE that we have from the island. A digital interactive database of the inscriptions analysed for this research is being developed as part of the project. Once completed, this will be an important resource for anyone interested in researching language contact, multilingualism and epigraphic habit in Roman Sicily.
